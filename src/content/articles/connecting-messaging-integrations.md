## What Are Integrations?

Integrations are the connections between SympleHost and the messaging platforms your guests use. Once connected, messages from WhatsApp, Instagram, or Facebook Messenger flow directly into your SympleHost **Messages** inbox — no more switching between apps to reply to guests.

Think of it like forwarding your mail to one address. Guests message you wherever they prefer; you see and reply to everything in one place.

---

## When to Connect an Integration

Set this up as soon as you've added your first listing. Messaging integrations are essential if you want to:

- See all guest messages in one unified inbox
- Use **Autopilot** to automatically respond to common questions
- Keep a complete conversation history for every guest

---

## Available Platforms

SympleHost currently supports three messaging integrations:

| Platform | What It Connects |
|----------|-----------------|
| **WhatsApp** | Your WhatsApp Business account — guests who message your business number |
| **Instagram** | Direct messages from your Instagram business profile |
| **Facebook Messenger** | Messages from your Facebook business page |

---

## How to Connect WhatsApp

WhatsApp is the most popular integration for vacation rental hosts. Here's how to set it up:

1. Go to **Settings** in the sidebar.
2. Click **Integrations**.
3. Find the **WhatsApp** card and click **Connect**.
4. Follow the on-screen authorization steps to link your WhatsApp Business account.
5. Once connected, the card will show a **Connected** status.

[Screenshot: The Integrations page showing the WhatsApp card with the "Connect" button]

After connecting, any message sent to your WhatsApp Business number will appear in your SympleHost **Messages** inbox. You can reply directly from SympleHost, and the guest will see your response in WhatsApp.

⚠️ **Important:** You need a WhatsApp Business account (not a personal WhatsApp account) to use this integration. If you don't have one yet, you can set one up for free through the WhatsApp Business app.

---

## How to Connect Instagram & Facebook Messenger

Instagram and Facebook are managed together since they're both part of the same platform:

1. Go to **Settings → Integrations**.
2. Find the **Facebook Integrations** card — this covers both Instagram and Messenger.
3. Click **Connect** and authorize with your Facebook account.
4. Once connected, you'll see toggle switches for **Instagram** and **Messenger** — enable the ones you want.

[Screenshot: The Facebook Integrations card showing toggle switches for Instagram and Messenger]

💡 **Tip:** You need to have an Instagram Business or Creator account linked to a Facebook Business Page for the Instagram integration to work.

---

## Verifying Your Connection

After connecting any platform:

1. Go to **Messages** in the sidebar.
2. Send a test message to yourself from the connected platform (e.g., message your WhatsApp Business number from a personal phone).
3. The message should appear in your SympleHost inbox within a few seconds.

If it doesn't show up, check the Troubleshooting section below.

---

## Troubleshooting

### Messages aren't appearing in my inbox
First, check that the integration still shows as **Connected** in **Settings → Integrations**. If the status has changed, try disconnecting and reconnecting. For WhatsApp, make sure the webhook (the behind-the-scenes connection that delivers messages in real time) was configured correctly during setup.

### I connected the wrong account
Go to **Settings → Integrations**, find the platform card, and click **Disconnect**. Then reconnect with the correct account.

### Instagram messages aren't coming through but Facebook is working
Make sure your Instagram account is a **Business** or **Creator** account (not a personal account) and that it's linked to your Facebook Business Page. You can check this in Instagram's settings under **Account Type and Tools**.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Connect at least one messaging platform before turning on Autopilot — without an integration, there are no messages for Autopilot to respond to
- WhatsApp is the most common starting point for rental hosts
- All connected messages appear in the unified **Messages** inbox
- You can connect multiple platforms at once — they all feed into the same inbox
