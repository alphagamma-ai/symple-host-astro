## What Are Services in SympleHost?

Services are anything you offer to guests **beyond the accommodation itself** — airport transfers, cooking classes, guided tours, spa treatments, cleaning, photography, and more. Each service gets its own booking page, pricing, availability calendar, and checkout flow.

Think of services as mini products that guests can browse and book, either during their stay or as standalone experiences. You can manage them yourself or link to an external partner who delivers the service.

---

## When to Use Services

- You offer **experiences or activities** — city tours, wine tastings, adventure excursions
- You provide **transport** — airport transfers, chauffeur services
- You sell **add-on services** — professional cleaning, private chef, photography
- You work with **local partners** who provide services to your guests
- You want guests to **book and pay online** through your direct booking page

---

## How to Create a Service

Creating a service is a 4-step process: Basics → Pricing → Availability → Policies. You can save after each step and come back later.

### Step 1: Set Up the Basics

1. Click **Services** in the sidebar, then click **Listings**.
2. Click **+ Add New Service** (purple button, top right).

![The My Services page showing existing service cards with the "+ Add New Service" button in the top right](/screenshots/creating-and-managing-services/my-services-page.png)

3. Fill in the **Basic Information**:

| Field | What to Enter | Required? |
|-------|--------------|-----------|
| **Service Name** | A clear, descriptive name — e.g. "Airport Transfer Service", "Half-Day London Highlights Tour" | Yes |
| **Category** | Select the best fit from the dropdown (see category list below) | Yes |
| **Location** | Where the service takes place — start typing and select from the autocomplete | No |
| **Service Description** | Describe what the service includes, what guests can expect, and any highlights. 20–2,000 characters. | Yes |

**Available categories:** Chef, Local Tours, Groceries, Experience, Activities, Chauffeur, Photographer, Trainer, Equipment Rentals, Cleaning, Childcare, Beauty, Spa, Other.

💡 **Tip:** Not sure what to write for the description? Click the **AI Enhance** button (the sparkle icon next to the description field). As long as you've filled in the service name and category, SympleHost's AI will generate a professional description for you. You can edit it afterward.

[Screenshot: The Basic Information form showing the Service Name, Category dropdown, Location field, and Description with the AI Enhance button]

4. Upload **Service Images** — drag and drop or click **Choose Files**. Supported formats: JPEG, PNG, WebP (up to 10MB each). The first image becomes the primary photo shown on your service card.

5. Click **Create Service**.

Your service is saved as a **Draft** and you're taken to the next step.

---

### Step 2: Configure Pricing

After saving the basics, you'll land on the **Pricing** page. Here you set how much the service costs.

#### Adding Service Rates

Each service can have one or more **rates** — different pricing tiers or packages. For example, a tour service might have a "Standard Tour" and a "Premium Private Tour" at different prices.

1. Click **+ Add Rate**.
2. Fill in the rate details:

| Field | What to Enter |
|-------|--------------|
| **Rate Name** | A label for this pricing tier — e.g. "Standard", "Premium", "Group of 4" |
| **Description** | What's included at this price point (up to 500 characters) |
| **Pricing Model** | How the price is calculated (see options below) |
| **Price** | The base price in your account currency |
| **Min Capacity** | Minimum number of guests required to book |
| **Max Capacity** | Maximum guests allowed per booking |
| **Notes** | Any additional pricing notes (optional, up to 500 characters) |

**Pricing model options:**

| Model | How It Works | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Flat Rate** | One fixed price regardless of guest count | £150 for a private transfer |
| **Per Person** | Price multiplied by number of guests | £25 per person for a walking tour |
| **Per Unit** | Fixed price per item or unit | £40 per kayak rental |
| **Per Group** | Fixed price for the entire group | £200 for a group cooking class |

3. Click **Save** on the rate.
4. Add more rates if needed — for example, a "Solo" rate and a "Group" rate.

[Screenshot: The Pricing page showing a rate card with the name, pricing model, price, and capacity fields filled in]

💡 **Tip:** You can reorder rates using the up/down arrows, and toggle individual rates on or off with the **Active** switch.

#### Optional: Add-ons and Discounts

You can also configure:

- **Add-ons** — extras guests can select during booking (e.g. "Packed lunch +£10", "Professional photos +£30"). Each add-on has a name, price, and optional description.
- **Discounts** — percentage or fixed-amount reductions (e.g. "Early bird 10% off").

Click **Next** or **Save** to continue.

---

### Step 3: Set Availability

This step controls **when** the service can be booked and **how** bookings work.

#### Choose Your Provider Type

First, tell SympleHost who delivers this service:

| Option | What It Means |
|--------|--------------|
| **I manage this service** | You (or your team) deliver the service directly. You control the schedule and availability. |
| **External partner** | A third-party vendor delivers the service. Guests are directed to an external booking link. |

#### If You Manage the Service

1. Select a **Booking Model**:
   - **Scheduled Slots** — guests book from available time slots you define
   - **On Demand** — guests can book instantly when requested
   - **Custom Arrangement** — booking details are arranged directly between you and the guest

2. Set the **Service Duration** — how long the service takes (hours and minutes, minimum 15 minutes).

3. Configure **Operating Hours** — the days and times when this service is available:
   - Click **+ Add Operating Hours**
   - Select the **days** (Monday through Sunday — you can select multiple)
   - Set the **Start Time** and **End Time**
   - Add more entries for different schedules (e.g. weekdays 9am–5pm, Saturdays 10am–2pm)

[Screenshot: The Operating Hours section showing a weekday schedule (Mon–Fri, 09:00–17:00) and a weekend schedule (Sat, 10:00–14:00)]

4. Set additional booking controls:

| Setting | What It Does |
|---------|-------------|
| **Min Time Between Bookings** | Buffer time between one booking ending and the next starting (e.g. 30 minutes for cleanup) |
| **Max Bookings Per Period** | Limit how many bookings you accept per day, per guest, per stay, or per slot |
| **Max Concurrent Requests** | How many bookings can happen at the same time |
| **Fulfillment Time** | How quickly you confirm bookings (instant, 15 min, 30 min, 1 hour, 2 hours) |
| **Min Lead Time** | How far in advance guests must book (e.g. 24 hours, 3 days) |

5. Fill in the **Requirements** section so guests know what to expect:
   - **What's Included** — bullet-point list of what the service covers (e.g. "Transport to and from hotel", "All equipment provided")
   - **What's Not Included** — anything guests should be aware isn't part of the service (e.g. "Meals and drinks", "Personal insurance")
   - **What to Bring** — items guests should have with them (e.g. "Comfortable walking shoes", "Sunscreen")
   - **What Guests Need to Know** — important information like fitness requirements or age restrictions
   - **Mandatory Forms** and **Form Links** — if guests need to fill in a waiver or consent form before the service

6. Optionally add **Custom Instructions** — any additional notes for the guest before or during the service.

#### If It's an External Partner

1. Enter **Vendor Booking Instructions** — how the booking works with your partner.
2. Add the **External Booking Link** — the URL where guests will be directed to complete their booking (e.g. `https://partner-tours.com/booking`).

Click **Next** or **Save** to continue.

---

### Step 4: Set Policies

The final step covers payment, cancellation, and AI settings.

#### Payment Policy

1. Toggle **Require Deposit** on if you want guests to pay a deposit at the time of booking.
2. If enabled, choose the **Deposit Type**:
   - **Percentage** — e.g. 25% of the total price
   - **Fixed Amount** — e.g. £50 flat deposit
3. Enter the **Deposit Amount**.

#### Cancellation Policy

Set up rules that tell guests what happens if they cancel:

1. Click **+ Add Cancellation Rule**.
2. For each rule, enter:
   - **Timeframe** — e.g. "More than 7 days before" or "Within 24 hours"
   - **Refund Percentage** — 0% (no refund) to 100% (full refund)
   - **Conditions** — any additional notes (optional)
3. Add more rules for different timeframes. A typical setup might be:
   - More than 7 days before → 100% refund
   - 2–7 days before → 50% refund
   - Less than 2 days before → No refund

[Screenshot: The Cancellation Policy section showing two rules — one for 7+ days (100% refund) and one for less than 48 hours (0% refund)]

#### AI Autopilot for Services

If you want Autopilot to handle guest inquiries about this service:

1. Toggle **Enable AI Autopilot** on.
2. Optionally enable **Pre-Service Communication** — Autopilot will send guests relevant information before their booking date.
3. Add an **Email Template** with placeholders like `{{service_name}}`, `{{guest_name}}`, and `{{booking_date}}` that get filled in automatically.

#### Custom Conditions

Add any other terms or conditions in the **Custom Conditions** field (up to 2,000 characters).

Click **Save & Finish**.

## Publishing Your Service

After completing all four steps, your service is saved as a **Draft**. To make it live:

1. Go to **Services → Listings**.
2. Find your service card.
3. Click **Publish**.

Your service status changes from **Draft** (yellow badge) to **Published** (teal badge), and it becomes visible on your public booking page.

[Screenshot: A service card showing the "Published" status badge in teal, with Edit, Archive, and Delete action buttons]

💡 **Tip:** Click **View Public Page** (top of the Services page) to see what guests see when browsing your services.

## Managing Your Service Workforce

If you have staff who deliver services (guides, drivers, instructors), manage them in the workforce section:

1. Go to **Services → Workforce** in the sidebar.

![The Service Workforce page showing members grouped by category with tabs for All, Tour Guide, Instructor, Therapist, Chef, Driver, and General](/screenshots/creating-and-managing-services/service-workforce.png)

2. Click **+ Add Member**.
3. Enter their **Full Name**, **Email**, **Phone**, and select a **Category** (Tour Guide, Instructor, Therapist, Chef, Driver, or General).

![The Add Workforce Member modal showing the Full Name, Email, Phone, and Category dropdown with all category options visible](/screenshots/creating-and-managing-services/add-workforce-member.png)

4. Click **Send Invite**.

Each workforce member gets a profile with performance stats, assigned services, and task tracking. You can filter the workforce list by category using the tabs at the top.

## Viewing Service Bookings

There are two ways to see your service bookings:

- **Services → Calendar** — a calendar view showing all service bookings across your services. Switch between Day, Week, and Month views.
- **Bookings → Services** tab — the combined bookings page filtered to services.

Click any booking to see full details: guest info, selected rate, add-ons, total price, deposit status, and special requests. From here you can **Confirm** or **Cancel** pending bookings.

## Service Statuses Explained

| Status | What It Means | Badge Color |
|--------|-------------- |-------------|
| **Draft** | Service is saved but not visible to guests. Still being set up. | Yellow |
| **Published** | Service is live and bookable by guests. | Teal |
| **Archived** | Service is no longer active but its data and booking history are preserved. | Gray |

You can archive a service instead of deleting it if you want to keep its history. Archived services can be unarchived at any time.

## Troubleshooting

### I can't publish my service — the Publish button isn't working
Your service may be missing required information. Check that all four steps are complete: basics (name, category, description), at least one active rate with pricing, availability settings, and at least one image. The system checks these before allowing publish.

### Guests can't see my service on the public booking page
Make sure the service status is **Published** (not Draft or Archived). Also check that at least one rate is marked as **Active**.

### I want to temporarily stop accepting bookings for a service
Archive the service — go to **Services → Listings**, find the service card, and click **Archive**. This removes it from the public page without deleting it. Unarchive it when you're ready to offer it again.

### How do I delete a service?
You can only delete a service if it has **no bookings**. If bookings exist, archive it instead. To delete, click the **Delete** icon (trash can) on the service card and confirm.

### My service shows a different currency than expected
Service pricing uses your account currency. Check **Settings → General** to verify your country and currency are set correctly.

## Key Takeaways

- Services are bookable experiences, transfers, or add-ons — separate from your rental listings
- The 4-step wizard covers: Basics → Pricing → Availability → Policies
- Use **multiple rates** for different pricing tiers or packages
- Set **operating hours** and **min lead time** to control when guests can book
- Services start as **Draft** — click **Publish** when everything is ready
- Use the **Workforce** section to manage guides, drivers, and other service staff
